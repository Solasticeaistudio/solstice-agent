# War Room Campaign Brief

## Objective

Use Solstice Agent's outreach stack to run a controlled outbound campaign for War Room, a strategic decision stress-testing service for high-stakes operators.

The goal is not broad awareness. The goal is to book paid pilot conversations with organizations that already understand:

- scenario analysis
- strategic uncertainty
- portfolio or mission risk
- executive decision pressure

Primary offer:

- Paid Decision Sprint

Decision Sprint output:

- executive verdict
- survivability score
- key vulnerabilities
- counterfactual interventions
- evidence-linked rationale
- concise executive debrief

This should be positioned as a fast, high-signal decision stress test that compresses weeks of analysis into hours.

## What War Room Is

War Room is a multi-engine strategic intelligence and war-gaming capability designed to pressure-test important decisions before they fail in the real world.

It is best framed as:

- strategic decision stress-testing
- counterfactual analysis
- vulnerability mapping
- board-ready scenario intelligence

Avoid framing it as:

- abstract AI consulting
- generic automation
- a broad transformation platform
- experimental research

## Ideal Buyers

Best near-term buyers:

- private equity portfolio operations
- value creation teams
- corporate strategy teams
- risk strategy leaders
- analytics leaders with client-facing decision products

Strong secondary buyers:

- reinsurance and risk intermediaries
- defense and mission decision teams
- large advisory and intelligence organizations

## Offer Ladder

Primary wedge:

- Paid Decision Sprint

Possible packaging:

- Rapid Decision Run
- Decision Sprint
- Full War Room
- Quarterly reassessment retainer

The first outbound motion should focus on the Paid Decision Sprint because it is easier to understand and easier to approve.

## Outreach Principles

- Be direct, credible, and specific.
- Do not sound like a generic consultant.
- Do not over-explain architecture in first contact.
- Lead with the buyer's decision pressure, not the engine stack.
- Optimize for routing when the contact is not the true owner.
- Keep initial emails easy to forward internally.
- Treat every outbound as draft-first unless explicitly approved for autonomous send.

## Success Criteria

The campaign is working if it produces:

- warm routing to strategy, portfolio ops, corp dev, or innovation
- replies asking for examples or sample outputs
- pilot conversations for an active decision
- evidence about which segment converts fastest

The campaign is not working if it produces:

- polite curiosity with no concrete next step
- confusion about whether this is consulting or software
- heavy interest from investor relations with no internal routing

## Hard Constraints

- Never fabricate customers, traction, pricing wins, or deployment scale.
- Never imply classified or unauthorized use cases.
- Never claim proprietary access to a prospect's internal data.
- Never position the service as replacing strategy teams.
- Never send attachments outside the approved campaign set.
