# War Room Email Sequence

## Sequence Overview

Use a five-touch sequence over fourteen days.

Touch order:

1. Initial offer
2. Concrete use case
3. Credibility and differentiation
4. Routing ask
5. Close the loop

Keep initial emails around 120-170 words. Follow-ups should be shorter.

## Touch 1: Initial Offer

Subject options:

- Rapid stress-test for high-stakes decisions
- Board-ready decision brief in hours
- Paid decision sprint for strategic decisions
- A faster way to pressure-test important calls

Body pattern:

Hi {first_name},

I built a strategic decision stress-testing system that produces a concise, board-ready brief in hours instead of weeks.

A typical sprint surfaces:

- executive verdict and survivability score
- key vulnerabilities and failure points
- counterfactual interventions
- evidence-linked rationale

I am offering this as a paid Decision Sprint for teams working through high-uncertainty decisions such as {segment_use_case}.

If useful, I can send a one-page sample output or outline a pilot. If I am off target, would you point me to the right person in {routing_targets}?

Best,
Justin

## Touch 2: Concrete Use Case

Subject options:

- Example use case for {company}
- Where this fits in your workflow

Body pattern:

Hi {first_name},

Following up with a more concrete use case.

For a team like {company}, I would position this as a rapid stress-test on {segment_use_case}. The goal is not a long consulting deck. It is a compressed decision brief that shows what breaks, what matters most, and what changes the outcome.

If helpful, I can send a sample outline or propose a small paid pilot around one active decision.

Best,
Justin

## Touch 3: Credibility

Subject options:

- Why I reached out
- Adversarial decision testing, not generic consulting

Body pattern:

Hi {first_name},

A quick note on why I think this is different.

My background is in building adversarial and analytical systems that pressure-test decisions, assumptions, and system behavior under uncertainty. The point is to surface vulnerabilities and second-order effects that normal planning processes often miss.

If useful, I can send a one-page sample before we talk.

Best,
Justin

## Touch 4: Routing Ask

Subject options:

- Who owns this internally?
- Quick routing question

Body pattern:

Hi {first_name},

I realize this may not sit with you directly.

If there is someone better for this internally, I would appreciate a pointer to the right owner in {routing_targets}. This is specifically for a paid Decision Sprint, not a broad transformation engagement.

Best,
Justin

## Touch 5: Close The Loop

Subject options:

- Close the loop?
- Should I pause?

Body pattern:

Hi {first_name},

I will close the loop after this.

If a rapid decision-stress-testing sprint would be useful at some point, I would be happy to reconnect. If there is someone else I should speak with, I would appreciate a pointer.

Best,
Justin

## Segment Tokens

### PE / Portfolio Ops

- segment_use_case: an active deal, a value-creation thesis, or a post-acquisition turnaround plan
- routing_targets: Portfolio Ops, Value Creation, or Corporate Development

### Risk / Data / Analytics

- segment_use_case: a client-facing scenario product, an internal strategic initiative, or a decision-intelligence pilot
- routing_targets: Strategy, Product, Innovation, or Corporate Development

### Insurance / Reinsurance

- segment_use_case: a portfolio exposure question, a hedging decision, or a catastrophe-related strategic move
- routing_targets: Risk Strategy, Innovation, or Corporate Development

### Defense / NatSec

- segment_use_case: a mission posture decision, program risk review, or strategic scenario exercise
- routing_targets: Strategy, Innovation, or Mission Engineering
